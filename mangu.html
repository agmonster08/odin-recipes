Mangu


Description

      Mangu is typical dish made through the caribbean, but is well recognized for being
      part of the Dominican Republic cuisine.
      I'ts a dish that it's mainly eaten during dinner time, even though in some places they
      also eat it for breakfast. It's a dish that's usually not eaten by it self, it's mostly
      eaten with salami, eggs and cheese. Many people also eat it with salami guisado.

Ingredients

  • 1-liter of water
  • 2-Green Plantains
  • 2-table spoons of oil,butter or margarine. you choose
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

In a sauce pan you will add your water, and put on the stove on medium high heat,
while the water is boiling you will take a pairing knife and peel your plantains.
Once peeled wash them in water to remove any dirt, add it tou your pan with a pinch
of salt. Let them boil for 15-20 minutes.
After boiling you plantains remove the water, just leave 4 tables spoons in the pot ,
afterwards use a cup, potato masher or fork and mash your plantain with butter or
what you decided on using, it should be smooth and grainy at the same time once you're
done.
